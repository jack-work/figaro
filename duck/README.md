# duck/

Rubber-duck brainstorms — captured thinking from design conversations,
preserved before the ideas are reduced to a plan or code. Lower commitment
than `plans/`, higher commitment than scratch notes.

## Convention

- One file per session, named `YYYY-MM-DD-topic.md`.
- A status header up top: `brainstorm`, `superseded`, `accepted` (folded
  into a plan), or `archived`.
- Lossy organization is fine, but the user's reasoning should be preserved
  in their own framing — not translated into a synthesis. If structural
  edits help readability, mark them.
- A "feedback" / "open questions" section at the bottom is welcome but
  must be clearly attributed and separable from the brainstorm itself.

The goal is *auditable thinking*: a reader two months from now should be
able to reconstruct why a decision was made, including the alternatives
the author considered and rejected.
